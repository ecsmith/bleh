GivJoy

Givjoy is a mobile application for allowing a person to bring joy to one of their friend. GivJoy does this by creating a marketplace for people with creative talents to be employed to bring joy to other people.

Sarah Park, a buisiness develoment posed this prompt in her initial pitch for the idea of GivJoy at the beginning of ProtoHack. This question really resonated with me and I decided to join their team and help them to solve their problem.

The Beginning

Going into ProtoHack I had no idea what to expect. As an ex-developer, the idea of "no coding" during a hackthon was something frightening to me. In my prior hackathons the day usually ending up in team frustration and no working code to show for it.

―To say the least I was anxious about what the day would bring. The turnout of ProtoHack mostly consisted of buisiness development professionals, UX designers and marketers. They gave us the morning of ProtoHack to build teams and comes up with ideas for a product. Many enthusiastic individuals also took to the stage to pitch their ideas to find talent to help them.

About ProtoHack

ProtoHack exists to show non-coders that they can create something amazing from nothing. It empowers and enables the non-technical community with the tools, knowledge and connections that will help bring their idea to life and visually communicate it through prototyping. Using prototyping tools, drag and drop editors, mockups, wireframes and anything else you think of to visually demo your idea on stage. Competitors will work in teams (up to 5) or solo. You have 9 hours to prototype your idea and then you’ll present on stage.
