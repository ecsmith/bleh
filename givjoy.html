GivJoy

Givjoy is a mobile application for allowing a person to bring joy to one of their friends. GivJoy does this by creating a marketplace for where-in a joy-sender can employ a person with a joy-giving creative talents to bring joy to their friend. GivJoy was created at ProtoHack, a code-free hackathon, in 7 hours.

FYI Sarah Park, a marketing specialist posed the above prompt in her initial pitch for the idea of GivJoy at the beginning of ProtoHack. This question really resonated with me and I decided to join their team and help them to solve their problem.

The Beginning

―To say the least I was anxious about what the day would bring. The turnout of ProtoHack mostly consisted of buisiness development professionals, UX designers and marketers. They gave us the morning of ProtoHack to build teams and comes up with ideas for a product. Many enthusiastic individuals also took to the stage to pitch their ideas to find talent to help them.

About ProtoHack

ProtoHack exists to show non-coders that they can create something amazing from nothing. It empowers and enables the non-technical community with the tools, knowledge and connections that will help bring their idea to life and visually communicate it through prototyping. Using prototyping tools, drag and drop editors, mockups, wireframes and anything else you think of to visually demo your idea on stage. Competitors will work in teams (up to 5) or solo. You have 9 hours to prototype your idea and then you present on stage.

My Role in our Team

Our ProtoHack team consisted of 5 individuals–3 Buisiness Development people and 1 other UX Designer besides myself. Besides 2 of the Biz Development none of us had met each other before.

Our Initial Steps

ProtoHack has Begun! Our initial step in the process was algning on the problem and identifying our problem. I proposed we try an activity to align our team on the problem we were trying to solve

Our Users have trouble finding a unique way to spread joy to friends & family. Its time consuming to find unique gifts for them.

Research

  1. Tell us about the last time you gave a gift to someone that wasn't a special occasion. What was their reaction?
  2. How did you know what to get that person? Where did you get it from?
  3. What was the most memorable gifts you have ever recieved not for an occasion? Why was it so special?
